# Security and privacy basics

A short, plain-language account of how the brain keeps content in the right hands.
It complements the [[Company handbook]] and the [[Welcome to Hyper Brain]] page.

## Domains are isolation boundaries

Every document belongs to a domain, and a domain is a hard boundary. You only ever
retrieve from domains your identity has been granted. Nothing crosses a boundary by
accident: even the link graph is kept inside a single domain, so following links can
never surface a document you are not allowed to see. The [[Glossary]] has the exact
definitions.

## Your personal space is yours

Notes you write land in a private space only you can read. It is never shared until
you deliberately share it, and you can revoke access at any time.

## Least privilege by default

New people start with the commons and their own personal space, and are granted team
domains as needed. Grants widen access; they never open a domain to everyone unless
that domain is the commons itself. Sharing is the one way content reaches another
person directly, covered in [[Sharing and collaboration]].
