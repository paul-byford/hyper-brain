# Sharing and collaboration

Most knowledge starts private and becomes useful when it reaches the right people.
Sharing is how you cross that gap on your own terms. Capture first (see
[[Writing good notes]]), then share.

## What you can share

- **A single note.** Hand one note to a colleague when that is all they need.
- **A whole space.** Give someone read access to your personal space so they see
  everything in it, current and future.

## It is always your choice

Sharing is explicit and reversible. You decide what to share and with whom, and you
can revoke access at any time; anything you never share stays private for good. This
is the deliberate exception to the least-privilege model described in
[[Security and privacy basics]].

## Shared with you

Anything a colleague shares with you appears alongside your own domains, scoped to
exactly what they granted, so you can search and answer across it like any other
space you can read.
