# Structured interview copilots

Structured interviews, where every candidate is asked comparable questions against
a defined rubric, predict job performance far better than unstructured chats and
are fairer. AI copilots make structure easier to sustain at enterprise scale.

## What the copilot does

Before the interview it proposes role-relevant questions tied to the rubric.
During the interview it helps the interviewer take structured notes against each
competency. Afterwards it drafts a rubric-aligned scorecard from those notes for
the interviewer to review and correct.

## What it must not do

It must not score candidates autonomously, infer protected characteristics, or
analyse a candidate's face or voice for personality: those cross into the
high-risk and often unlawful territory described in
[[Bias audits and hiring compliance]]. The interviewer owns the assessment; the
copilot organises evidence.

## Fitting the funnel

The copilot picks up the shortlist from [[AI sourcing and candidate matching]] and
operates under the data rules in [[Candidate data privacy and consent]]. Its value
is consistency and better notes, not a shortcut around human judgement.
